01 / Second Chance

Second Chance
Money Skills

Justice-Involved Adults

One of the only active jail-based financial literacy programs in South Texas. Second Chance Money Skills meets participants inside correctional facilities and reentry programs — where trust is built through consistency, and where practical financial skills have the greatest impact on long-term outcomes.

About
Making quality financial education accessible to everyone — regardless of income, background, or circumstance.

LearnTheMoney is a Texas-based financial literacy initiative delivering free, research-backed financial education through community partnerships and direct program delivery. We serve justice-involved individuals rebuilding their financial footing, adult learners seeking economic stability, and educators who will shape the next generation's financial understanding. Our model is simple: if the education is good enough and delivered with care, it works — regardless of who's in the room.

Ali Ibrahimov

Founder · LearnTheMoney

Ali Ibrahimov founded LearnTheMoney on a straightforward conviction: that financial knowledge is not a luxury — it is a prerequisite for economic participation. A Financial Analyst at Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi, he brings together the discipline of a working institutional analyst and the curiosity of a researcher to design programs that genuinely change financial behavior.

Pursuing a PhD in Curriculum & Instruction at TAMUCC and an A.L.M. in Finance at Harvard Extension School, Ali works at the intersection of behavioral finance, education research, and community development. His scholarship is not confined to journals — it is tested directly in the communities he serves: Nueces County correctional facilities, community learning centers, and teacher preparation programs across Texas.

His body of work includes peer-reviewed publication on AI-supported financial literacy education, a pending patent for a Transaction-Triggered Financial Literacy Intervention System — a technology platform designed to deliver financial guidance at the moment of spending decisions — and the founding of LearnTheMoney's jail-based program, one of the only active initiatives of its kind in South Texas.

Ali was selected to speak at TEDxSST, presenting on behavioral finance and community-based financial education to an audience of educators, policymakers, and practitioners. In 2026, he was recognized with the Making an Impact Award (MAIA) for his contributions to financial literacy education and community outreach across Texas.
